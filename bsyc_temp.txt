<h1 class="entry-title">Top 50 Master’s in Computer Science Degree Programs (Updated)</h1>
</header><!-- .entry-header -->
<div class="entry-content">
<div class="wp-block-image">
<figure class="alignright"><a href="https://www.computersciencedegreehub.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/CSDH-TDP.jpg"><img alt="CSD Top Degree Programs" class="wp-image-13151" decoding="async" height="300" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" src="https://www.computersciencedegreehub.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/CSDH-TDP_1-300x300.png" srcset="https://www.computersciencedegreehub.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/CSDH-TDP_1-300x300.png 300w, https://www.computersciencedegreehub.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/CSDH-TDP_1-150x150.png 150w, https://www.computersciencedegreehub.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/CSDH-TDP_1-1024x1019.png 1024w, https://www.computersciencedegreehub.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/CSDH-TDP_1.png 2000w" width="300"/></a></figure></div>
<p>By CSDH Staff</p>
<p>It’s a great time to pursue a master’s degree in computer science! Not only is the average salary of an employee with a master’s degree $101,000 — much higher than those with only a bachelor’s — but the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics reports that employment in computer science occupations is expected to grow a whopping 15% through 2029. That’s way faster than the average industry! We guess <i>Forbes </i>was right when it declared the master’s degree to be the new industry standard!<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>But it’s not easy to determine which colleges and universities are best when it comes to where to study for a master’s in computer science. There are many factors to consider, including overall cost, national reputation, and curriculum, to name but a few considerations. That’s why Computer Science Degree Hub took on the task of determining the top 50 master’s in computer science degree programs, updated for 2021.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p><em><strong>Ranking Methodology</strong></em></p>
<p>To come up with our ranking of the best master’s degrees in computer science, we began by making a list of more than 75 programs throughout the United States. We considered only those universities offering a distinct master’s degree in computer science, as opposed to a track or concentration. To narrow down our list to the top 50, we used the following points system.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p><strong>Annual Graduate Tuition &amp; Fees (Estimate):<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></strong></p>
<p>Under $70,000 — 2 points<br/>Under $60,000 — 3 points<span class="Apple-converted-space"><br/></span>Under $50,000 — 4 points<span class="Apple-converted-space"><br/></span>Under $40,000 — 5 points<span class="Apple-converted-space"><br/></span>Under $30,000 — 6 points<span class="Apple-converted-space"><br/></span>Under $20,000 — 7 points<br/>Under $10,000 — 8 points<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p><strong>Annual Percentage of 20-Year Net Return on Investment (ROI):</strong><span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>1% and below — 0 points<span class="Apple-converted-space"><br/></span>2-3% — 1 points<span class="Apple-converted-space"><br/></span>4-5% — 2 point<br/>6-7% — 3 points<br/>8-9% — 4 points<span class="Apple-converted-space"><br/></span>10% and above — 5 points<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p><strong>Average Early-Career Salary of Graduates:</strong><span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>$50,000 and below — 1 point<br/>$60,000 and below — 2 points<br/>$70,000 and below — 3 points<br/>$80,000 and below — 4 points<br/>$90,000 and below — 5 points<span class="Apple-converted-space"><br/></span>$100,000 and below — 6 points<br/>Above $100,001 — 7 points<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p><strong>National Recognition:</strong><span class="Apple-converted-space"><br/></span>Note: To ensure consistency, we consulted only those applicable rankings produced by <i>U.S. News &amp; World Report </i>(Best National University Rankings, Best Computer Science Schools), <i>Wall Street Journal/Times Higher Education College Rankings </i>(U.S. College Rankings), and <i>Forbes </i>(America’s Top Colleges, Best Master’s Degree: Computer Science).</p>
<p>Top 100 — 1 point<br/>Top 50 — 2 points<br/>Top 40 — 3 points<br/>Top 30 — 4 points<br/>Top 20 — 5 points<br/>Top 10 — 6 points<br/>Top 5 — 7 points<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>Please note, in the case of varying information (for example, a difference in tuition for state residents and non-residents), points were allocated based on the highest number of points possible. Ties were broken at the discretion of the editors.</p>
<h3>1. <a href="https://www.computersciencedegreehub.com/colleges/cornell-university/" rel="noopener">Cornell University</a><br/>Master of Science in Computer Science</h3>
<div class="wp-block-image">
<figure class="alignright"><img alt="" class="wp-image-12949" decoding="async" height="150" loading="lazy" src="https://www.computersciencedegreehub.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/01/Cornell-University-150x150.png" width="150"/></figure></div>
<p><b>Points: </b>40<br/><strong>2020 Ranking: </strong>1<br/><strong>Tuition:</strong> $29,500</p>
<p>Located in Ithaca, New York, Cornell is a private Ivy League university. The Master of Science in Computer Science from Cornell is a 34-hour program which takes four semesters to complete. Students are required to turn in a written thesis and give an oral presentation about the thesis results. Elective course topics include:</p>
<ul>
<li>blockchains</li>
<li>compilers</li>
<li>open-source software engineering</li>
<li>matrix computations.</li>
</ul>
